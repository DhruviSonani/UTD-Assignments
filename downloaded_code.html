Frequently Asked Questions

Where do tutoring sessions take place?

We have worked over Zoom for years, far predating the wider societal shift during the pandemic. We have mastered the art of relationship-building at a distance, so while we look forward to a world in which we are all working together in person again, we are well-positioned to serve your needs now.

What ages do you work with?

We generally work with students in grades K-12, but also routinely work with college students and adults. Our tutors have taught everything from Kindergarten to college level Physics and the GRE.

What are your hourly rates?

Our rates vary depending on organizational goals and needs. After an initial consultation we will provide an appropriate quote.

What is academic coaching?

Our mentors do so much more than just help students with homework. The right mentor will support students through their most challenging classes and academic decisions, but also their most challenging life decisions. In this capacity, our tutors improve a student’s confidence, strengthen their study and organizational skills, but also increase their overall well-being. In short, we take a holistic approach and never lose sight of it.

What are your tutors' qualifications?

Simply put, our mentors are the best of the best, with hundreds of years of combined experience in college prep, admissions, test prep, and academic tutoring. We come from a variety of backgrounds – some of us have PhDs in science, others JDs and formal teaching degrees – but we are united by our passion for mentoring students.

How do I get started?

You can get started now by using our online intake form. You may also reach us via email at info@tutorpedia.com or through our online contact form. Alternatively, call us at 415-484-2833 to talk about your needs and to get a specific recommendation for a tutor that will be a good match for your child. Please allow 24 hours for us to get back to you.
